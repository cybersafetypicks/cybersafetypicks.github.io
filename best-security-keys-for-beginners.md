# Best Security Keys for Beginners

## Who This Is For:
This guide is for non-technical users looking to secure their accounts without needing extensive technical knowledge.

## What To Look For:
When choosing a security key (also known as an authentication token or backup key), consider the following:
- **Ease of Use:** The tool should be easy to set up and use.
- **Compatibility:** Ensure it works with your devices (like smartphones, computers).
- **Security Level:** Higher security levels mean more secure communication between you and your accounts.

## Recommended Options:
1. **Yubico YubiKey**
   - *Why:* Easy setup; works on most platforms including Windows, macOS, iOS, Android.
2. **Google Authenticator Mobile App**
   - *Why:* Works well with Google accounts (no need to buy a physical key), but requires your own smartphone.

## Mistakes To Avoid:
- Buying the most expensive security key immediately without trying simpler ones first.
- Choosing features that are not necessary for basic use, like hardware wallets or biometric authentication systems, which might be too complex and confusing for beginners.

## Final Recommendation:
For non-technical users just starting to secure accounts, **Yubico YubiKey** is highly recommended due to its straightforward setup process and compatibility with popular devices. While the Google Authenticator mobile app is also suitable if you already own a smartphone, it may not suit all situations since it requires your own device.
