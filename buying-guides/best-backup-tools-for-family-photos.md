## Who This Is For:
This guide is for non-technical users looking to back up their family photos in a simple and easy way.

## What To Look For:
When choosing a backup tool for your family photos, look for these key features:

- **User-Friendly Interface:** No need to be tech-savvy – the tool should be easy to use.
- **Automatic Backups:** The software should automatically save new photos so you don’t have to remember or click anything.
- **Secure Storage:** Ensure that your family photos are safe and not easily accessible by others.

## Recommended Options:
Here are some backup tools for families:

### 1. Google Photos
Google Photos allows you to store unlimited high-quality photos and videos, automatically backs up new uploads, and offers secure cloud storage. Users can also manage their backups through a simple web interface.

### 2. Microsoft OneDrive
Microsoft OneDrive stores your family photos in the cloud so they’re accessible from any device with internet access. It’s user-friendly for non-technical users and comes with automatic backup features.

### 3. Dropbox Family Cloud
Dropbox Family Cloud also allows you to back up all of your family photos automatically. The service offers easy file management, secure storage, and integration with popular cameras and printers.

## Mistakes To Avoid:
Here are some common pitfalls to avoid:

- **Ignoring Free Versions:** Some services offer free versions that may have limitations on photo sizes or number of backups.
- **Not Using Encryption:** Ensure the backup tool uses strong encryption for your sensitive family photos. While this feature is not always required, it’s a good safety measure.

## Final Recommendation:
For most non-technical users, Google Photos and Microsoft OneDrive are highly recommended due to their ease of use and automatic backup features. They also offer robust security measures that protect your family's digital memories from unauthorized access.
