# What is Two-Factor Authentication and Why It Matters?
## Who This Is For:
Non-technical users who want to understand the basics of two-factor authentication (2FA) without delving into complex technical details.

## What To Look For:
Two-factor authentication adds an extra layer of security beyond your password by requiring a second form of verification. This usually comes in the form of something you know (like a PIN or password) and something you have (like a code sent to your phone).

## Recommended Options:
1. **Text Message Codes**: Simple and widely supported, codes are sent to your mobile device.
2. **Authentication Apps**: Utilize apps like Google Authenticator that generate time-based one-time passwords (TOTPs).
3. **Biometric Authentication**: Features such as fingerprint or facial recognition if available.

## Mistakes To Avoid:
- **Not Using 2FA at All**: Leaving out even a simple extra step can make your account more vulnerable.
- **Relying Solely on Text Messages**: SMS codes are less secure because they can be intercepted, which is why many apps offer alternative methods like authenticators or biometrics.

## Final Recommendation:
Use two-factor authentication to add an extra layer of security to your accounts. Choose a method that fits comfortably with you and is widely supported by sites where it’s available.
