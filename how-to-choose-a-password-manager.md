# How to Choose a Password Manager in 2026

## Who This Is For:
This guide is for non-technical users looking to manage their passwords more securely and easily.

## What To Look For:
When choosing a password manager, consider the following:

1. **Ease of Use**: The tool should be straightforward to set up and use.
2. **Security Features**: Choose one with strong security features like encryption.
3. **Storage Options**: Pick one that suits your needs—local storage or cloud-based.
4. **Privacy Settings**: Ensure they protect your personal data and privacy.

## Recommended Options:
1. **LastPass** - Easy to set up, good security features, works on multiple devices.
2. **Dashlane** - Known for its ease of use and comprehensive security options.
3. **1Password** - Offers strong encryption and multi-factor authentication (MFA).

## Mistakes To Avoid:
- **Neglecting Security Features**: Don’t pick a password manager that lacks proper encryption or MFA.
- **Overlooking Privacy Settings**: Ensure the service respects your privacy settings and doesn't share data without consent.

## Final Recommendation:
Choose **LastPass** for its ease of use and good security features. If you're concerned about privacy, consider **1Password**, which offers strong encryption and has robust privacy settings.
