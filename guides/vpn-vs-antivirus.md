VPNs and antivirus software are both security tools, but they protect you
from very different threats. Many people need both.

## What a VPN Does

A VPN encrypts your internet traffic and masks your IP address. It protects
your **connection** — particularly on public Wi-Fi — and limits how much your
internet provider or websites can track your browsing.

**Protects against:** Eavesdropping on public networks, IP tracking, some
forms of surveillance.

**Does NOT protect against:** Viruses, malware, phishing links, or malicious
downloads.

## What Antivirus Does

Antivirus software scans your device for malicious files and programs. It
protects your **device** from threats that reach it.

**Protects against:** Viruses, ransomware, spyware, trojans, some phishing
attempts.

**Does NOT protect against:** Network-level surveillance or IP tracking.

## Do You Need Both?

For most home users: **yes**. They complement each other.

| | VPN | Antivirus |
|---|---|---|
| Encrypts internet traffic | ✅ | ❌ |
| Blocks malware | ❌ | ✅ |
| Hides IP address | ✅ | ❌ |
| Scans downloads | ❌ | ✅ |
