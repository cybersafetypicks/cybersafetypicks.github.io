## For Whom:
This information is intended for non-technical users who want to understand the basic differences between a Virtual Private Network (VPN) and an antivirus software.
## What To Look For:
When choosing cybersecurity tools, it’s important to know what each one does. A VPN encrypts your internet traffic, allowing you to browse privately and securely from any location. An antivirus protects against malware by scanning and removing malicious files or programs.
## Recommended Options:
- **For a good antivirus:** Consider using an up-to-date antivirus software like Avast, Bitdefender, or Norton. Regularly update these as new threats are discovered.
- **For enhanced privacy:** A strong VPN service is offered by NordVPN, ExpressVPN, and Surfshark. They help mask your online activities to protect your identity from prying eyes.

## Mistakes To Avoid:
- **Don’t** use a single tool for everything: Some people try to get away with just using an antivirus or only a VPN. Both are essential but each serves different purposes.
- **Avoid** being too choosy and not protecting yourself enough by avoiding any cybersecurity tools all together.

## Final Recommendation:
For most users, having both an updated antivirus software and a trusted VPN service is ideal. This combination helps secure your online activities from various threats including malware and unauthorized access, while also providing you with greater privacy when accessing the internet.
